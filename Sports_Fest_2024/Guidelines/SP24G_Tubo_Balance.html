Tubo Balance

Written by:

PE 4 Committee

Category:

Recreational Games

Date published:

21 April 2024

Tubo Balance pits ten players in a race to delicately balance a plastic tube on their index finger and be the first to stand it upright atop a central pole. Divided into teams, players relay the tube between poles while adhering to strict rules prohibiting any contact beyond a single finger. With precision, strategy, and teamwork, competitors navigate the challenge, aiming for the triumphant moment of tube-on-pole perfection.

Team/Player Composition

10 players (all genders)

Type of Tournament

Team Play

Game Mechanics

  • Per team need to decide who are the first five player who brings the tube thru pole B and the last five player needs to finish the race
  • To start the game, all players should agree on a starting signal, such as a countdown or a whistle.
  • When the game begins, all players pick up their tubes and balance them on their index finger.
  • The players should then walk towards the pole while keeping the tube balanced on their finger. If the tube falls off the player's finger, they must stop and pick it up and balance it again before continuing.
  • Once a player reaches the pole, they should try to carefully place the tube on top of it. The tube should be standing straight up, with the base of the tube touching the pole and the players from pole B will continue the race going back to their pole A
  • The player who successfully places the tube on the pole and makes it stand up straight on its base is the winner.
  • If a player drops the tube or knocks it over, they must start again from the beginning.

Rules

  • Players must balance the tube on one finger only.
  • Players may not touch the tube with their other hand or any other part of their body.
  • Players must stay within the designated playing area.
  • If a player violates any of these rules, they must start again from the beginning.

How to win

The objective of the game is for players to balance a long, narrow tube with one finger and race to the pole at the other end of the playing field. The first player to reach the pole with the tube balanced and standing upright is the winner.

Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • The do’s are all listed in the “How to play” section.

Don'ts

  • Don't touch the tube with any part of your body other than your index finger.
  • Don't use your other hand to stabilize or balance the tube.
  • Don't step out of the designated playing area.
  • Don't start the race before the agreed-upon starting signal.
  • Don't rush or run while carrying the tube.
  • Don't knock over or drop the tube during the race.
  • Don't continue racing if the tube falls off your finger; stop and pick it up to rebalance.
  • Don't interfere with other players' attempts to balance their tubes.
  • Don't place the tube on the pole until you've reached it with the tube balanced.
  • Don't lean or tilt the tube excessively while trying to place it on the pole.
