Darts is a competitive sport, wherein players are tasked to throw pointed projectiles at a round board called a dartboard. There are many ways to play darts, but the game chosen is a modified version of killers, where no points accumulates and the goal is to close all required houses/numbered sections.

Team/Player Composition

1 boy, 1 girl

Type of Tournament

Single Elimination

Material/Equipment

  • Dart pins (players must bring their own)

Mechanics

  • Players take turns to throw 3 darts each at the dartboard.
  • Players must throw darts from a certain distance (9ft) or behind the line (Do not remove the darts until your score is written down).
  • Players must hit the numbered sections of the board at least 3 times to close a house/number.
  • Hitting a double with a single dart counts as hitting a house twice, Triple is thrice, immediately closing the number.
  • Bull/Cherry cannot be closed until players have closed the numbers 15 and above.

How to win

Players must close all numbers 20-12 and the Bull by hitting a house 3 times. This can be achieved faster by hitting doubles and triples as they multiply your hits on a number.

Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • Stand behind the oche or line marking the distance.
  • Find a grip that is comfortable and natural for you.
  • Take a proper stance, one that doesn’t throw you off balance.
  • Take your time to aim between throws.
  • Aim for different segments if you still have remaining darts on your turn.
  • Keep quiet during someone’s turn.
  • Be respectful towards other players.

Don'ts

  • Do not interfere with other players' throws.
  • Do not throw multiple darts at the same time.
  • Do not step beyond the distance mark when throwing.
  • Do not add excessive force when throwing.
  • Do not aim for the cherry/bull until the numbers 20 – 12 are closed.
