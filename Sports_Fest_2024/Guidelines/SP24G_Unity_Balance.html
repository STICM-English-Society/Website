Unity Balance

Written by:

PE 4 Committee

Category:

Recreational Games

Date published:

21 April 2024

Unity Balance is a thrilling team-based challenge where players must work together to balance a bottled water on a compact disc suspended by 10 strings, each held by a team member. Teams race against the clock to navigate the precarious setup from the starting line to the finish, using coordination and communication to avoid spilling the water. The team with the shortest completion time emerges victorious in this test of balance and teamwork.

Team/Player Composition

10 players (all genders)

Type of Tournament

Single Elimination

How to play

Game setup

  • Prepare a flat surface large enough to accommodate multiple teams.
  • Place a compact disc (CD) at the center of the surface.
  • Attach a bottled water on top of the CD using adhesive putty or another secure method.
  • Arrange 10 strings around the CD, evenly spaced, and tie one end of each string to the CD. The other end of each string is held by a player.

Team formation

  • Divide players into teams, ensuring each team has an equal number of members.
  • Assign each player to hold one string attached to the CD. Each player uses only one hand to hold the string.

Starting line

  • Designate a starting line where all teams will begin the game.
  • Ensure there's enough space between the starting line and the CD to allow for movement during the game.

Game objective

  • The objective of "Unity Balance" is for teams to navigate the CD and bottled water from the starting line to a designated finish line without dropping or spilling the water.
  • The team that completes the course in the shortest time wins the game.

Gameplay mechanics

  • At the start signal, teams begin moving the CD and bottled water by coordinating the movement of the strings held by each player.
  • Players must work together to maintain balance and stability, adjusting their movements as needed to prevent the water from spilling.
  • Teams can communicate and strategize to navigate obstacles and challenges along the course.
  • If the water spills or the CD falls off the strings, the team must restart from the starting line.

Timekeeping

  • Use a stopwatch or timer to record the completion time for each team.
  • The timer starts when the start signal is given and stops when the CD and bottled water cross the finish line.
  • Keep track of each team's completion time for scoring purposes.

How to win

  • The team with the shortest completion time wins the game.
  • If multiple teams complete the course without dropping the water, the team with the fastest time is declared the winner.

Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • The do’s are all listed in the “How to play” section.

Don'ts

  • Don’t let go of the strings
    • Each player must hold onto their assigned string throughout the game to maintain control and stability over the CD and bottled water.
  • Don't rush
    • Avoid making sudden movements or rushing through the course, as it can lead to instability and increase the risk of spilling the water.
  • Don’t forget to communicate
    • Effective communication is essential for coordinating movements and adjustments among team members. Don't hesitate to communicate with your teammates to ensure smooth navigation of the CD and water bottle.

If one of these violations occurs, 5 seconds will be added to the team’s completion time each time they violate one of the don’ts.
