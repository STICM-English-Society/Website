E-Games

Date published:

21 April 2024

This tournament is for the game Tekken and is called the Tekken Sports Fest Tournament. Players will compete in intense battles to showcase their skills and emerge as champions of the tournament.

Team/Player Composition

  • 1 SHS representative (all genders)
  • 1 Tertiary representative (all genders)

Type of Tournament

Round Robin

  • The tournament follows a Round Robin format within each group for qualifying. The top 2 teams from each group will advance to the semi-finals, while the last-place team from each group will be eliminated. The winners from each group will compete for 1st place, and the runners-up will compete for 3rd place.

Materials/Equipment

  • Controller or arcade sticks
    • Players must bring their own controller or arcade stick to play the game.
  • Laptop
    • Also bring laptop for the game.

How to play

  • Each match will be a Best of 3, meaning the first player to win 2 matches will be declared the winner. In case of a tie, an instant elimination match will be held to decide the winner.
  • The finals will be a First to 5 matches to win, determining the overall champion.
  • Each round will have a 60-second timer. Games will be a Best of 5 setting, requiring 3 rounds to win. The first game will feature a random stage.
  • The loser of each game can change characters and select the stage, while the winners can only use the character previously selected.
  • Winners can only use the character previously selected.
  • Winners can only change characters after each phase of the tournament if they continue winning.

How to win

To win, players must perform their best and win the required number of matches to progress in the tournament. The goal is to win the final match and be crowned the champion.

Do's and Don'ts

  • Players must bring their own controller or arcade stick to the tournament.
  • The maximum waiting time for an opponent is 10 minutes. If the opponent does not show up within this time, they will automatically forfeit the match.
  • Players must maintain a respectful and sportsmanlike environment throughout the tournament. Unsportsmanlike conduct, cheating, or intentional disruption of the tournament may result in disqualification.
  • Spectators are allowed but must respect the players and not disrupt or distract them during the matches.
  • Officiators have the authority to remove spectators if they engage in excessive trash talk towards players.
  • In the event of technical issues, such as a blackout or PC crashing, the system will revert to the previous state of the last complete match. Rounds won before the technical issue will be wiped, and the match will start again to 0 â€“ 0.
  • If a controller is disconnected during the game, the match will be paused, and the opposing team can decide whether to restart or continue the match.
  • Each player has a one-time timeout per match-up, which can only be used during character select and not during gameplay.
