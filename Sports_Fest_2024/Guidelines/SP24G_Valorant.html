Valorant

Written by:

PE 4 Committee

Category:

E-Games

Date published:

21 April 2024

This tournament is for the game Valorant, a popular competitive first-person shooter. Teams will compete against each other in matches to showcase their skills, teamwork, and strategy.

Team/Player Composition

Each team should consist of 5 main players and 1 reserved player. All team members should be students of STI College Marikina.

Type of Tournament

Single Elimination with a fair randomizer

  • This means that teams will compete in a bracket-style format, where each match is a direct elimination. Once a team loses a match, they will be eliminated from the tournament.
  • The matchups in the early rounds of the tournament will be randomized, ensuring that teams face different opponents in each round. This randomization helps create a fair and unpredictable tournament experience, where any team has a chance to succeed. The team that emerges as the winner of the final match will be declared the overall champion of the tournament.

Materials/Equipment

  • Own peripherals
    • Participants may bring their own peripherals, such as their own keyboard, mouse, mousepad, and headset/earphones.
  • No internet sharing devices
    • The use of routers or any internet sharing devices, including mobile hotspots, is not allowed.
    • This rule is to ensure that each player has an independent and dedicated internet connection, which helps maintain a smooth gameplay experience for everyone.

How to play

  • A map will be chosen randomly, and teams will proceed to play heads or tails to determine which team plays as the attacker or defender.
  • The game mode will be Standard, and the lobby setup will be a 5 vs. 5 custom game.
  • Players must adhere to the rules and regulations of the game, including not playing under another player’s account or Riot ID and not using hacks or third-party programs.

How to win

The winning conditions depend on the specific match. However, in the finals, if there is a tie at 12:13, the game will continue until one team has a 2-round lead over the other.

Do's and Don'ts

  • All participants must treat each other and the opposing team with respect.
  • Using game bugs, exploits, or glitches for an advantage is strictly prohibited.
  • Playing under another player’s account or Riot ID is not allowed.
  • Hacks or other third-party programs are prohibited and may result in disqualification.
  • Teams are allowed pause breaks and are required to inform the administrators about technical issues.
  • Coaches are not allowed to communicate with players for tactical purposes during technical timeouts.
  • Disconnected players have a limited time to reconnect, or the team may have to continue playing without them.
  • Lag issues can be addressed during the prep phase, and if not resolved within a certain time frame, the team may need to continue playing with the current state.
  • In the event of a server crash, the game can be continued from a new draft, but the lineup must remain the same.
  • If you are using an internet connection for the game and it gets disconnected or lost, the game will need to be restarted.
