Running Tic Tac Toe

Written by:

PE 4 Committee

Category:

Recreational Games

Date published:

21 April 2024

Running Tic Tac Toe is a dynamic team game where five players per team compete to strategically place X or O markers on a large ground-based Tic Tac Toe board. Players sprint to the board, place markers, then race back to their team's line to high-five the next player, aiming to be the first team to achieve three markers in a row horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. With scoring based on Tic Tac Toe wins and potential penalties for infractions, the game offers fast-paced excitement and strategic fun.

Team/Player Composition

5 players (all genders)

Type of Tournament

Round Robin

Game Mechanics

Game Setup

  • There is a large Tic Tac Toe board laid out on the ground. Each square of the board should be large enough for a player to stand in.

Game Start

  • On the signal to start ("go"), the first player from each team runs down to the Tic Tac Toe board.

Placing Markers

  • Each player places their team's marker (e.g., X or O) in one of the squares of the Tic Tac Toe board.

Returning to line

  • After placing the marker, the player races back to their team's line and high-fives the next player in line.

Sequential placement

  • The next player in line then repeats the process, placing their team's marker in an open square on the board.

How to win

The goal is for a team to get three of their markers in a row horizontally, vertically, or diagonally on the Tic Tac Toe board.

Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • The do’s are all listed in the “How to play” section.

Don'ts

  • Failure to high-five

    • If a player fails to properly high-five the next player in line after placing their marker, their team may incur a penalty, such as a delay in their next turn or loss of turn.

  • Unsportsmanlike conduct

    • Any form of unsportsmanlike behavior, such as taunting opponents, using inappropriate language, or intentionally disrupting the game, may result in penalties ranging from loss of turn to disqualification from the match.

  • Interference

    • Players must not interfere with the placement of markers by opposing players or impede their movement back to the team's line.
    • Interference could result in penalties such as loss of turn or forfeiture of the match. Example of this is the overlap of markers from the opposing team.
