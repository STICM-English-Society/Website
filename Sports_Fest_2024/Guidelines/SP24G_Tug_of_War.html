Tug of War

Written by:

PE 4 Committee

Category:

Recreational Games

Date published:

21 April 2024

Tug of War brings together teams of eight players on each end of a marked rope, with the objective of pulling the designated spot over their team's line. With three rounds per match, teams compete to win the most rounds, showcasing strength and teamwork. Violations such as wrapping the rope around body parts, sitting while pulling, or improper form may result in penalties, ensuring fair play and competitive excitement for all participants.

Team/Player Composition

8 boys, 8 girls

  • Games for boys and girls are separated.

Type of Tournament

Double Elimination

Game Mechanics

Teams

  • Two teams consisting of 8 players both on different ends of the rope.

Objective

  • Pull the marked spot of the rope over your team's line.

Scoring

  • The team that has successfully pulled the marked spot of the rope over their line will be declared the winner of the round.

The gameplay consists of only one round.

How to win

To win in tug of war, teams must utilize teamwork, proper technique, and strategic planning. This involves coordinating pulls, maintaining a firm grip on the rope, and leveraging leg strength for maximum force. Endurance, focus, and adherence to fair play are also crucial for success, ensuring teams can outlast opponents and achieve victory through determination and sportsmanship.

Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • The do’s are all listed in the “How to play” section.

Don'ts

  • Wrapping the rope around a body part
    • Wrapping the rope around any body part (e.g., hands, elbows, arms etc.) will be considered a violation.
  • Sitting on the ground while pulling
    • Players must be standing up while pulling on the rope and must not be on the ground in any form while pulling.
  • Fail of form
    • Players must pull the rope from under their arms and their elbows must not go below their knee.
