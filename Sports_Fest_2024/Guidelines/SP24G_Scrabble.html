Scrabble

Written by:

PE 4 Committee

Category:

Recreational Games

Date published:

21 April 2024

Scrabble is a word game where players use lettered tiles to create words on a game board. Each player starts with a rack of seven tiles bearing letters with corresponding point values. Points are scored by forming words and strategically placing tiles on the board to utilize bonus squares. The game continues until all tiles have been drawn and one player has used all their tiles, or when no more words can be formed. Scrabble combines vocabulary, pattern recognition, and strategic thinking, offering both casual enjoyment and competitive challenge.

Team/Player Composition

2 boys, 2 girls

Type of Tournament

Team Play

Game Mechanics

  • The game has a total duration of only 40 minutes. Each player has 10 minutes to make their moves.
  • All players draw seven letter tiles from the tile bag to form their initial rack. The remaining tiles are placed face down in the tile bag.
  • Players take turns forming words on the game board. The first player combines two or more of their tiles to form a word on the board's center square, which is marked with a star.
  • Words can be placed horizontally or vertically, with each new word connected to existing words on the board. Tiles must be placed in a straight line and cannot overlap existing tiles unless forming a new word.
  • After placing a word, the player tallies the points for each letter used, based on their assigned values. Additionally, certain squares on the board multiply the word or letter scores, such as double or triple letter or word score squares.
  • After each turn, players replenish their racks to seven tiles by drawing from the tile bag.

How to win

To win, you must consider scoring, time management, and accumulating points on the board. A team color who has the highest score at the end of the game wins.

Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • Place your tiles on the game board in a neat and orderly fashion while forming words.
  • Always refill your rack into 7 tiles after each turn until no tiles are left inside the tile bag.
  • Always check your score for every word you make.
  • Merriam-Webster dictionary will be the sole authoritative source for verifying the validity of words.

Don'ts

  • Do not place tiles outside the boundaries of the game board.
  • Do not disrupt the placement of tiles already on the board unless you're forming a valid word.
  • No validation or asking of words within the game.
  • Do not use tiles from previous turns to form new words unless they're part of the word you're extending.
  • Players must refrain from playing words that they know are invalid or do not exist in the English language.
  • Bringing mobile phones into the play area or engaging in any form of cheating will automatically be considered a violation of the guidelines.
  • No other dictionaries or reference materials will be permitted for use in determining the acceptability of words played during the game.
