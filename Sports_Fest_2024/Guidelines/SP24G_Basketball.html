Volleyball is a team sport where players hit a ball over a net using their hands. The goal is to score points by getting the ball to touch the ground on the opponent's side of the court.

Team/Player Composition

  • Maximum: 12 Males and 12 Females
  • Minimum: 8 Males and 8 Females

Team pairings

  • Brown and Gray
  • Violet and Blue
  • Red and Green

Collaboration System Collaboration will be implemented in volleyball games, where the committee will pair two colors to form one team. The pairings are being decided through observation to ensure fairness and equal opportunity for every team.

Type of Tournament

  • Round Robin

Game Mechanics

Serve

  • Serve must come from behind the end line or the restraining line until after contact.
  • t is possible to serve the ball overhand or underhand.
  • If the served ball hits the net, it can fall to the other side for a point.
  • As soon as the official blows the whistle for the serve, the player has 8 seconds to start the serve.

Rotation

  • Each time a team wins the serve, they will switch rotation.
  • The players must only turn in a clockwise direction.
  • There will be six people on each side.

Timeouts

  • Every set has a 2-minute break for each team.
  • The referee calls a technical timeout, which lasts one minute, when the team in the lead gets to sixteen points in each set.
  • Only the coach or, if the coach isn't there, the game leader can call a timeout.
  • If one of the teams reached 8 points, they will change court and that would be considered their timeout.

Substitutions

  • Teams can make as many substitutions as they want during a set, but players can only join or leave the game in a certain area designated for substitutions.

Net Contact

  • To play the ball, players can reach above the net and not touch it.

Tally Points

  • The champion team will earn 6 points, the first runner-up will receive 5 points, the second runner-up will be awarded 4 points, the 4th-place team will earn 3 points, the 5th-place team will receive 2 points, and the 6th-place team will be awarded 1 point.

How to win

  • Every time the ball hits the ground, a point will be given.
  • If the defense misses or hits the ball out of bounds, the offense will score.
  • For elimination round: In sets 1 and 2, the game will be having 25 points. The first team who will win the first two (2) sets will win the match.
  • For the championship, the match will have 5 sets, with 25 matching points the first team who wins the first three set will take the championship.
  • If the championship match reaches the fifth set, then the set will only have 15 matching points to decide the winner. The winner should have 2 points lead against the opponent.
