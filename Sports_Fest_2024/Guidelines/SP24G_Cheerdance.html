Date published:

Cheer dance is a competitive performance sport that combines elements of dance, jumps, formations, and teamwork to create an engaging routine without relying on risky stunts. A yell must be included before executing the routine.

Team/Player Composition

Minimum: 20 participants (all genders)
Maximum: 30 participants (all genders)

  • Members who are listed in the list should not back-out before or during the practice/event.

Material/Equipment

  • STI Marikina Sports Fest T-shirt

    • If the team decides to use costumes for their performance, wearing the official Sports Fest 2024 T-shirt on the day of the competition is optional. However, participants must still wear their designated colored official Sports Fest 2024 T-shirt on all other days of the event.

  • Music

    • Each team must submit their music to the committee a day before the performance, to avoid rushing on the day of the performance. The music should be appropriate and complement the choreography.

  • Props

    • Select the props that are suitable for the routine and do not hinder the performers’ movements. Props will be checked by the committees a day before the performance to ensure they are safe to use.

  • Official Timer

    • The committee will provide an official timer to track the time consumed by each group during their performance. Each team can assign one representative to be with the timer assigned by the committee to witness the timekeeping process.

Mechanics

The mechanics focuses on the safety during practice, avoid any risky movements, emphasizing the use of dance elements, planning, and executing formation changes carefully, choosing appropriate music, selecting suitable props, and having a minimum of 20 and maximum of 30 participants.


The performance has a minimum duration of 3 minutes and a maximum of 4 minutes.


The team shall follow the guidelines on stunts and liftings, and a failure to do so is equivalent to a 10-points deduction on the final score.


If the team exceeds the maximum number of participants, there will be a 1-point deduction. 3 points will be deducted to their final score if there is 1 participant that is nonexistent, and a deduction of 5 points if there are 2 participants that are nonexistent and so on.


Rules

  • Prioritize safety by avoiding risky movements such as:

    • Allowed movements:
      • Motion
      • Clasp
      • High-V
      • Jumps
      • Thigh-stand
        • Allowed as long as it won’t hurt anyone.
    • Prohibited movements:
      • Backflip
      • Shoulder stands or stepping on members’ shoulders.
      • Throwing members in the air.
      • Complex pyramid
      • Tumbling passes
      • Handstands
      • Extreme flexibility moves
      • Cartwheeling
      • Aerial tricks
      • Risky moves without proper training and supervision.

  • Performance must be level 1 only.

  • Focus on dance elements, formations, and teamwork to create an engaging routine. Emphasize the use of jumps and other formations.

  • Plan and execute formation changes carefully to maintain visual appeal and spatial awareness throughout the performance.

  • Select the appropriate music that complements the choreography and enhances the overall performance. Avoid relying solely on the music to carry the performance. Avoid sudden or extreme volume changes, as it can disrupt the flow of the routine and makes it challenging for the performers to stay synchronized. Avoid overly complex or fast-paced music, as it can make it difficult for the performers to keep up with the music and execute their movements accurately.

Criteria for Judging

PE 4 Committee

How to win

  • Following the safety guidelines and avoiding risky movements.
  • Executing well-choreographed routines that emphasize dance elements, formation, and teamwork.
  • Maintaining visual appeal and spatial awareness through carefully planning and execution of formation changes.
  • Selecting appropriate music that complements the choreography and enhances the overall performance.
  • Showcasing effective teamwork by showing the synchronization among the team members in their performance.
  • Adhering to the rules and guidelines set by the competition organizers.
  • Remember, the decision of the judges is final and irrevocable in determining the winner of the competition.

Do's and Don'ts

  • DO prioritize safety during practices.
  • DO emphasize dance elements, formations, and teamwork.
  • DO select appropriate music and props.
  • DON'T perform risky movements like backflips or throwing members in the air.
  • DON'T exceed the maximum number of participants.
  • DON'T exceed the time limit.
  • DON'T rely solely on music for the performance.
  • DON'T include inappropriate words in yells.
  • DON'T interrupt the performance unless necessary due to technical issues or injury.
