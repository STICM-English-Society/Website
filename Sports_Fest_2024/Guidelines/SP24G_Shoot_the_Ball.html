Shoot the Ball

Written by:

PE 4 Committee

Category:

Recreational Games

Date published:

21 April 2024

Shoot the Ball is a dynamic team game where players must balance and guide a ball on a tarpaulin, aiming to land it in designated holes for points. Effective communication and teamwork are crucial as players maneuver the ball using the tarpaulin's movements. With two rounds of one-minute durations, teams compete to earn the most points, emphasizing skill, coordination, and fair play.

Team/Player Composition

5 players (all genders)

Type of Tournament

Single Elimination

Game Mechanics

  • Roles such as "holder" and "guide" may be assigned to team members. There will be 4 “holders” and 1 “guide” for each team.
  • Game setup
    • Teams use a tarpaulin to hold and balance a ball. Players, specifically “holders" will be blindfolded and must use both hands to lift the tarpaulin and ball together, practicing moving the ball around by tilting the tarpaulin in different directions with the help of “guide”.
  • Communication
    • Effective communication among team members is crucial. Players should talk and give feedback to each other to maintain balance and control of the ball.
  • Balancing the ball
    • The objective is to balance the ball on the tarpaulin and guide it to land in the appropriate colored hole without falling into the same hole twice.
  • Scoring
    • Points are earned based on where the ball lands
      • Landing in the near hole earns 2 points.
      • Landing in the distant hole earns 5 points.
      • No points are awarded if the ball lands in the incorrect color.
  • Game rounds
    • The game consists of two rounds, each with a one-minute time limit.

How to win

The team with the most points at the end of both rounds wins the match.

Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • The do’s are all listed in the “How to play” section.

Don'ts

  • Use of unauthorized equipment
    • Teams must use only the provided tarpaulin and ball. Use of additional or unauthorized equipment may result in deduction of points.
  • Interference
    • Players from one team must not interfere with the other team's attempts to balance the ball. Intentional interference may result in penalties or disqualification.
  • Penalties will be applied, resulting in a deduction of 1 point each time a player did any of the listed don’ts.
