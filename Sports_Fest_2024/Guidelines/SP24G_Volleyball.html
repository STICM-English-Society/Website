Volleyball Boys and Girls

Written by:

PE 4 Committee

Category:

Sports

Date published:

11 April 2024

Volleyball is a team sport where players hit a bill over a net using their hands. The goal is to score points by getting the ball to touch the ground on the opponent's side of the court.

Team / Player Composition

  • 14 players maximum
  • 3 Boys & 3 Girls

Type of tournament

  • Single elimination

Game Mechanics

Serve

  • Serve must come from behind the end line or the restraining line until after contact.
  • It is possible to serve the ball overhand or underhand.
  • If the served ball hits the net, it can fall to the other side for a point.
  • As soon as the official blows the whistle for the serve, the player has 8 seconds to start the serve.

Rotation

  • Each time a team wins the serve, they will switch rotation.
  • The player must only turn in a clockwise direction.
  • There will be 6 people on each side.
  • 3 girls on the frontline and 3 boys on the backline.

Timeouts

  • Every set has a 2-minute break for each team.
  • The referee calls a technical timeout, which lasts one minute, when the team in the lead gets to 16 points in each set.
  • Only the coach or, if the coach isn't there, the game leader, can call a timeout.
  • If one of the teams reaches 8 points, they will change court and that would be considered their timeout.

Net contact

  • To play the ball, players can reach above the net and not touch it.

Tally points

  • Champion: 6 points
  • First runner-up: 5 points
  • Second runner-up: 4 points
  • Fourth place: 3 points
  • Fifth place: 2 points
  • Sixth place: 1 point

How to win

  • Every time the ball is hit, a point will be given.
  • If the defense misses or hits the ball out of bounds, the offense will score.
  • In sets 1 and 2, the game will go to 21 points. Set goes to 15 points with a two-point lead to decide the winner.

Do's and don'ts

  • It is a foot fault if any part of either foot touches the line or the court beyond the line before the ball is hit.
  • A player serving out of turn or out of the correct rotational order is considered a rotational fault in volleyball.
  • Touching the net with anything other than the ball or a player's hair will automatically be a violation, and the opposing team will get a point.
  • Male players in the front line are not allowed to spike. They are only allowed to spike in the backline.
