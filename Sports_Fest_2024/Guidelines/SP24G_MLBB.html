Mobile Legends: Bang Bang

Written by:

PE 4 Committee

Category:

E-Games

Date published:

21 April 2024

This game is a competitive tournament where teams compete against each other to destroy the opposing team’s base. It requires strategic gameplay and teamwork to emerge victorious.

Team/Player Composition

Each team must consist of 5 main players and 1 reserved player.

  • Gender composition can be mixed, all-male, or all-female, depending on the participants’ preferences.

Type of Tournament

Single elimination

  • Teams will compete in a bracket-style format, where each match is a direct elimination.
  • Once a team loses a match, they will be eliminated from the tournament. The matchups in the early rounds of the tournament will be randomized, ensuring the teams face different opponents in each round.
  • The team that emerges as the winner of the final match will be declared the overall champion of the tournament.

Materials/Equipment

  • Own device
    • Each participant should bring their own devices to play the game.
  • Own load
    • Players are required to have their own load to ensure a better connection during the game. This helps maintain a stable and uninterrupted gameplay experience.
  • No internet sharing devices
    • The use of routers or any internet sharing devices, including mobile hotspots, is not allowed.
    • This rule is to ensure that each player has an independent and dedicated internet connection, which helps maintain a smooth gameplay experience for everyone.
  • One device only
    • Each player is allowed to only use one device, such as a phone, tablet, or laptop, for playing the game. This rule helps maintain fairness and consistency among all participants.

How to play

Teams will compete against each other to destroy the opposing team’s base. They will need to strategize, communicate, and work together to outplay their opponents. The gameplay will involve using in-game abilities, tactics, and coordination.

Pause time

  • If a player disconnects during a Top 8 match hosted by the admins, they can request a pause from an MDC official. The maximum pause time for one time is 5 minutes.

Server crash

  • In the event of a server crash, the game can be continued from a new draft, but the lineup must remain the same.

No internet connection

  • If there is no internet connection within 10 minutes during an actual game, the game needs to be restarted from the same battle.

How to win

The winning condition is for a team to successfully destroy the opposing team’s base before the other team does. The team that achieves this goal first will be declared the winner. Alternatively, if a team surrenders, they will be considered the losers.

Do's and Don'ts

DO conduct yourself with sportsmanship.

  • Players are expected to maintain a friendly and polite demeanor towards other participants. Treat others with respect and fairness throughout the tournament.

DO strictly enforce the rules.

  • Team should ensure that all members adhere to the tournament rules and guidelines. This includes following the game mechanics, equipment requirements, and conduct rules

DO communicate and coordinate.

  • Effective communication and teamwork are key to success in the tournament. Coordinate strategies, share information, and work together as a team to achieve victory.

DO respect the game and its outcome.

  • Accept the outcome of each match with grace, whether it results in a win or loss. Show respect towards the game and the efforts of other teams.

DON’T use force outside the game.

  • It is strictly prohibited to use force or engage in any behavior outside the game that may influence the outcome. This includes engaging in physical altercations or any form of harassment. Such behavior can lead to severe punishments, including disqualification.

DON’T cheat.

  • Cheating in any form, such as wall hacking, piloting, or abusing bugs, is strictly prohibited. Engaging in cheating activities will result in automatic disqualification for all involved parties.

DON’T agree to lose on purpose.

  • Intentionally losing a match is considered a violation and can lead to disqualification. Maintain fair play and compete to the best of your abilities.

DON’T violate the equipment and connection rules.

  • Follow the guidelines regarding the use of personal phones, load requirements, and the prohibition of sharing internet devices. Violating these rules can disrupt the smooth flow of the tournament and may result in penalties.
