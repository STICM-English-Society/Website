IT101-A Students visit the Concepcion Uno Barangay Hall to extend help to the victims of the recent fire

Written by: Elmer Felisilda

Marikina, Philippines

January 25, 2023

As a Final performance task and exam for the subject National Service Training Program 1, the students of IT101-A organized a short outreach program for the victims of the recent fire that occurred in the Barangay earlier this year. The outreach occurred last January 18 and was held in the basketball court atop the Barangay hall that has been turned into a temporary evacuation area. The class donated relief goods to three chosen families who were then asked for an interview after.

The students packing the canned goods on its way to the barangay hall

Taken by: Elmer Felisilda

Preparations for said outreach program was made at January 11, as students were assigned their roles for the program by the core leaders of the class, Alexander Oro and Nyx Galera. Some students were assigned as carriers of the relief goods, while some were tasked to do a photo and video documentation of the program. The remaining students were tasked to donate goods such as canned food, instant noodles, and hygiene kits.

IT101-A student wearing the STI NSTP shirt

Taken by: Elmer Felisilda

Early in the morning, January 18, the day of the outreach program. Numerous students who is taking part on the actual program at the Barangay hall arrived and assembled at their classroom as early as 7:00 AM at STI College Marikina. Many have brought their last-minute donations before they go on their way to the evacuation area. At 7:45 AM, both Alexander and Nyx arrive at the classroom for their remaining announcements to organize the class. The relief goods was packed into eco-bags, and hauled into the car provided by Nyx. The remaining students go home since their tasks were finished, while Elmer Felisilda, JP Sanchez, Kiro Fernandez, and others more walked a short distance into the evacuation area along with their NSTP instructor Mr. Kurt Cruz.

The students along with their instructor arrives on the evacuation area where the fire victims temporarily live

Taken by: Elmer Felisilda

Arriving at around 8:15 AM, students arrived at the basketball court where the evacuees stay until the recover from the recent fire. The relief goods for the 3 families assigned to the section were immediately given, which the victims receive gratefully. A representative of the 3 families were asked by Mr. Cruz and the core leaders for a short video interview. All three representatives agreed and were given the interview questions ahead of the shooting.

Three interviewees being interviewed by Mark Darryl Matandag

Taken by: Elmer Felisilda

Directed by Reniel Baldove, he and Aerol Jacob Epino begins shooting the video as Mark Darryl Matandag interviews them. They were asked about what they felt during the disaster. One of the victims said that they were traumatized by the incident, unable to move and think as she were unable to save any stuff from her house. Another shared the same sentiment, as she says that her mind went blank at that moment. They were grateful that their Barangay Captain supported them as foods were given to them as they stay at the evacuation area.

Interviewer Darryl Matandag, Core Leader Alexander Oro, and Director Reniel Baldove
